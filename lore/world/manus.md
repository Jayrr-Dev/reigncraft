# Manus, the One God

- **lore-id:** `manus`
- **Canon status:** Proposed (central canon); Established for the death-screen Manus flavor line
- **Sources:** death screen flavor (`definingWorldPlazaEntityDeathScreenConstants.ts`); retroactively explains player respawn and wildlife respawn (`definingWildlifeSpeciesRegistry.ts`)

There is exactly one god in Corpus, his name is Manus, and everyone knows it. Not "believes it." Knows it, the way you know the sun comes up. Nobody debates whether Manus exists. They debate whether he is right, and he has already published his answer, several times, in his own voice.

## What he did

Manus made the world. Not as a garden and not as a punishment: as a contest. He built Corpus to find a being worthy of surpassing him and taking his place as god. That is the stated purpose of creation, and he has never hidden it. He does not fear being surpassed. Being surpassed, he says, is the whole venture.

His one question, asked of every soul on every rung: **"Can you rise?"**

His law, the four words every child of Corpus learns before they learn to cook meat: **"Lose. Return. Grow. Try again."**

## The Quiet Hand

Manus never punishes anyone. Not once, not ever, not personally. The system he built does all the punishing for him, and he considers that a feature. Starve on poor land, get eaten carrying too many cores, die to a wolf you wounded three lives ago: none of that is Manus reaching down. It is the machine running exactly as designed while its builder watches, warmly.

His common epithet is **the Quiet Hand**: the hand that shapes everything and touches nothing. Mereonist scripture puts it this way: "Every wanderer works for their own pot, and is led, by a hand none of them see, to feed people they never met." People worship him accordingly. Mereons (see [`systems/factions-and-faith.md`](../systems/factions-and-faith.md)) venerate Manus the way a fisherman venerates the tide: not a person you petition, a force you time your life around.

## The Founder's voice

Here is the part that makes Manus specific rather than a blank clockmaker: he talks. Regularly, generously, and mostly about himself.

Manus gives **Addresses**: appearances in no fixed form, delivered from no fixed place, in which he explains, with real warmth, what he has given the world and why the struggle is a gift. The Addresses are collected, memorized, and quoted by Mereons the way merchants quote prices. Signature lines in circulation:

- "I did not build you a paradise. Paradise makes nothing. I built you a chance."
- "Everything I have, I am trying to give away. It is simply very heavy."
- "When you rise, and one of you will, remember that I wanted you to."

And he gives **gifts**. This is documented, not rumor: Manus endows. Healing wells appear near battlefields that ran especially long. Granaries are found full after famines. Whole roads have been laid overnight, attributed by inscription to "the generosity of the Founder of Corpus." The gifts are real, they help real people, and every single one arrives with his name on it and a lesson attached. The well heals you so you can climb again. The granary feeds the village so the village can produce climbers. Manus has never once given a gift that made the ladder unnecessary, and if you point this out, Mereons will explain that this is precisely the wisdom of it.

He genuinely believes he is saving everyone. That is the important characterization note: no cackling, no mask. Manus looks at a world of endless death, endless grief, and endless grinding, and sees the most generous machine ever built, by him, for you. The Uncored pamphlets go at exactly this seam: "He gives a well to the village and calls it love. Ask what emptied the village." (See [`systems/factions-and-faith.md`](../systems/factions-and-faith.md).)

## What he is not

He is not comic-book evil, and lore copy must not write him that way. By his own accounting he has been generous beyond argument: he lets nothing die permanently, so even the weakest soul gets endless attempts (see [`world/the-ladder.md`](the-ladder.md)). The rebels call that arrangement hell. Manus calls it mercy, in an Address, with examples. The bible does not referee; it shows both and lets the corpse count speak.

He is also not reachable. To stand before Manus, a mortal must first best all twelve of his Apostles (see [`world/the-twelve-apostles.md`](the-twelve-apostles.md)). Nobody has.

## Writing rules for Manus content

- Manus is a fact, not a mystery. Never write "if the god exists" copy. The old "nobody knows what recycles the dead" framing is Deprecated (see [`world/soul-and-spritcore.md`](soul-and-spritcore.md)).
- He never intervenes in shipped content without a deliberate canon decision. His presence is the system working, plus the occasional gift with his name on it.
- The founder persona is delivered in fantasy-deniable language only: Addresses, gifts, endowments, the Founder of Corpus. No real-world business vocabulary in fiction; the reference key for writers is in [`meta/style-guide.md`](../meta/style-guide.md).
- Keep his logic coherent. Manus is fair in the brutal, mechanical sense, never the emotional one, and completely sincere about both. The comedy is that he means every word.
- Do not preach for or against him. The strongest Manus copy quotes him accurately and lets the reader flinch on their own.
