# The Firelands Ruins

- **lore-id:** `firelands-ruins`
- **Canon status:** Established (ruin types and props), Proposed (the failed-climb interpretation)
- **Sources:** `definingWorldPlazaFirelandsRuinBlueprintConstants.ts`, `definingWorldPlazaFirelandsBiomeConstants.ts`

The only pre-wanderer structures in Corpus. Four ruin types survive in the Firelands, all attributed to the Forgewrights. Under the current canon they read as the remains of the last civilization-scale attempt to climb past the Apostles (see [`world/history.md`](../world/history.md)). In-game copy should still flag interpretation as interpretation.

## Forge camps

**Contains:** lava forges, lava anvils, scattered fencing.

The Forgewrights worked metal with the land's own heat, tapping lava directly instead of building furnaces. The anvils are scaled for human-sized hands, the strongest evidence they were roughly people-shaped.

Interpretation: an armory at this scale is not subsistence smithing. They were equipping for something, and the camps sit close to ember vents in defensible spots, which suggests they worked under guard, or under threat.

## Portal shrines

**Contains:** lava portals, totems, obelisk fragments.

Gate structures, unmistakably deliberate, all currently dark. No wanderer has activated one, and the bible does not yet say whether they can be (see [`meta/open-questions.md`](../meta/open-questions.md)).

Interpretation: gates are routes, and routes in this world are owned (see [`world/the-twelve-apostles.md`](../world/the-twelve-apostles.md)). A people building their own gates was a people done paying tolls. Wanderer rumor splits the usual way: the optimists say the Forgewrights left through them ahead of the end; the pessimists say the answer to their ambition arrived through them.

## Obelisk circles

**Contains:** lava obelisks and totems arranged in patterns.

Standing stones in deliberate circular layouts. They do nothing observable.

Interpretation: apparatus for working soul energy in bulk. A civilization trying to out-climb the Apostles would need cores at a scale no battlefield provides, and the circles are the likeliest machinery for it. What they actually did (condensed cores, moved them, spent them) is unrecorded. Wanderers who camp inside a circle report nothing unusual, then usually mention they would not do it again. Keep this at the level of unease, never confirmed effects.

## Bastion settlements

**Contains:** the largest ruin footprints, walls, fencing, mixed props.

Fortified settlements. Walls face outward in all directions, which means the Forgewrights did not know where the answer would come from, or it came from everywhere.

Interpretation: you do not fortify like this against wolves. The bastions are the strongest physical evidence that the Forgewrights knew exactly what they were attempting and what attempting it invites.

## Writing rules for ruin content

- The ruins are evidence, not exposition. Describe what is physically there; let characters argue about meaning.
- No intact Forgewright text, art, or remains. The absence of bodies has an explanation now (nothing stays dead; see [`world/the-ladder.md`](../world/the-ladder.md)), but no individual has ever been identified as a returned Forgewright, and copy must not do so without a canon decision.
- New Firelands props should be attributable to one of these four ruin types, or explicitly natural.
