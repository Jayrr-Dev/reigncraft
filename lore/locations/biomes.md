# The Twelve Biomes

- **lore-id:** `biomes`
- **Canon status:** Established (biome list, rarity, codex summaries, hazards), Proposed (lore notes)
- **Sources:** `definingWorldPlazaBiomeKind.ts`, `definingPlazaBiomesGuideConstants.ts`, `definingPlazaBiomesGuideForagingConstants.ts`, `definingWildlifeBiomeSpawnTable.ts`

Rarity doubles as a danger and distance signal: common biomes cluster near spawn, legendary ground is a journey. Lore notes below are hooks for flavor text and future codex entries, not fixed canon until used in-game.

## Common

### Plains

Open grasslands with scattered wildflowers. The default world, and where most wanderers learn to survive. Herds of cows, sheep, and deer make it the safest larder in Corpus, which is why the first claims almost always go up here.

### Forest

Dense trees, shade, and quiet ground cover. Wood-rich and welcoming by day. By night it belongs to the wolf packs, and old hands say you can tell a new wanderer by whether they camp inside the treeline or outside it.

### Flower Forest

Bright meadows packed with colorful blooms. The prettiest ground in Corpus and weirdly under-fought-over. Wanderer superstition holds that it is bad luck to build war camps somewhere this nice.

### Snowy Plains

Frozen ground and biting cold air. The frost here slows the body toward a full stop; the physics-minded point out that cold has a floor (absolute zero) and the snowy plains seem determined to demonstrate it. Huskies, penguin-kin, and the well-dressed pass through comfortably. Everyone else hurries.

## Uncommon

### Beach

Soft sand where land meets open water. Neutral ground by unspoken custom. People fight over forests and plains; almost nobody fights over sand.

### Savanna

Dry grass and scattered acacia groves. Zebra country, and therefore lion country. The prides here hunt in coordinated groups, and the savanna is where most wanderers first learn that some animals plan.

### Rocky

Bare stone flats with sparse vegetation. Poor food, rich stone. Quarry ground for builders and a natural fortress for anyone willing to haul their food in.

## Rare

### Desert

Hot dunes with little shade or water. The scorch here is honest: it tells you it is killing you the whole time. Zebras shrug it off, which annoys everyone.

### Swamp

Murky wetlands with lily pads and reeds. The water looks calm because crocodiles are patient. The swamp is where Corpus teaches ambush: the danger you see in the badlands, you do not see here.

### Badlands

Layered clay hills and steep red drops. Beautiful, hot, and unforgiving about falls. Wanderers come for the views and the isolation; the ones who stay tend to like both more than company.

### Ocean

Deep water far from any shore. The rim of the known world. Nobody has reported another shore. That sentence is doing a lot of work and should keep doing it.

## Legendary

### Firelands

Scorched earth, ember vents, and ash, running a constant 62 degrees. The only biome with a history: the Forgewrights lived and died here (see [`world/history.md`](../world/history.md)), and their ruins are the reason anyone risks the heat. Detailed in [`locations/firelands-ruins.md`](firelands-ruins.md).

## Hazard vocabulary

Keep these terms consistent across lore and UI copy, matching the mechanics guide:

- **Scorch**: ambient heat damage (desert, badlands, Firelands).
- **Frost**: ambient cold that slows and then kills (snowy plains).
- **Burn**: direct lava contact.
