# Wanderers

- **lore-id:** `wanderers`
- **Canon status:** Established (avatar roster, respawn, innate traits); Proposed (ladder framing, the non-combatant premise); Deprecated (the "unexplained arrival, never answer it" rule)
- **Sources:** `registeringWorldPlazaCharacterEngineDefinitions.ts` (avatar roster), frost mechanics copy ("any character or NPC")

"Wanderer" is the bible's term for player characters. Use it in codex and flavor copy wherever "the player" would break fiction.

## Waking

Every wanderer's story starts the same way: waking near spawn with empty hands and no memory of a before. The canon explanation now exists. Manus throws new souls onto the ladder, and the ladder does not issue histories. You arrive as what you are, on the bottom rung, and the climb is the biography.

Wanderers know this, the way everyone knows Manus. It does not make waking less disorienting. The standing joke is that the only thing Manus gives you free is the question, and the question is "Can you rise?"

The old rule that arrival must never be explained is Deprecated. What stays deliberately blank is the personal past: no wanderer remembers a before, and copy must not invent one.

## The many shapes of wanderers

Wanderers do not all look human. The current roster: a girl, a husky, a golden retriever, a grizzly, a penguin, a peach fox, and an orange cat. All are people in the full sense: they talk, build, claim, and cook, unlike the true wildlife of Corpus, which never speaks.

The in-fiction framing: souls arrive wearing different bodies, and nobody finds this strange because everyone woke up the same way. Some shapes carry their nature with them; the husky and penguin do not feel the frost, and the grizzly shrugs off bleeding and carries a ward against heat. Wanderers treat these traits as facts about a person, like being tall.

Rule for new characters: each shape may carry one or two innate traits that make ecological sense for the animal. No shape is a different species in fiction; they are all wanderers.

## The one who cannot fight (Proposed)

The planned protagonist premise, all Proposed until it ships: the main character is a non-combatant. They can run, they can jump, and that is the complete list of martial skills. What they can do, better than anyone, is make friends.

In a world where the faith says you are what you can take, a person who cannot take anything is a walking theological problem. The non-combatant climbs anyway, through others: friends fight, chop, build, and haul, and the wanderer plays as the friends they have made (see [`species/npcs-and-friends.md`](npcs-and-friends.md) for befriending, settlement, and the permadeath tension). Mereonist doctrine does not have a comfortable shelf for this. The Uncored find it hilarious and slightly suspicious, since their insult "Corekeeper" still lands on whoever holds the ledger, whoever swung the axe.

## Culture

Wanderer culture is young and mostly self-made, which the lore should embrace:

- **Knowledge is the inheritance.** Which meat cooks safe, which biome kills, what a Cucco is: it travels by telling. Codex copy is written in this voice, a veteran wanderer explaining things to a newer one.
- **Claims are the social contract.** Territory norms are covered in [`systems/claims-and-realms.md`](../systems/claims-and-realms.md).
- **Death is weather.** Routine, discussed casually, planned around, and never quite comfortable. Everyone knows whose arrangement it is now, which changed the jokes but not the habit of making them.
- **The faiths are furniture.** Most wanderers are lazy Mereons the way most people are lazy anything: they use the vocabulary of merit when they win and skip services otherwise. Committed Mereons and Uncored both exist and both recruit (see [`systems/factions-and-faith.md`](../systems/factions-and-faith.md)).

## Writing rules

- No wanderer remembers a personal before, and copy never invents one. The ladder explains the arrival; it does not backfill the person.
- Avoid "hero" and "chosen" framing. Nobody in Corpus is special by decree; the ladder's whole pitch is that anyone could rise and almost nobody does.
- Player-built structures are wanderer-built in fiction. Never attribute buildings outside the Firelands to anyone else until NPC settlement ships.
