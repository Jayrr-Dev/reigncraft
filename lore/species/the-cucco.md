# The Cucco

- **lore-id:** `the-cucco`
- **Canon status:** Established (aggressive chickens, Cucco Rage, Crazy Chicken Meat), Proposed (folklore framing)
- **Sources:** `definingWildlifeVariantMeatRegistry.ts`, `definingWildlifeVariantMeatItemDescriptionCorpus.ts`, `definingWorldPlazaEntityDiseaseRegistry.ts` (cucco-rage)

Most chickens in Corpus are ordinary birds. Some are not. The aggressive spawns attack on sight, fight with a fury completely out of proportion to their size, and have earned a separate name in wanderer speech: **the Cucco**.

## The folklore

The Cucco is the most beloved cautionary tale in Corpus, and the lore should treat it with the same mix of comedy and genuine respect the players do. The standard telling:

> A Cucco is not angry at you. A Cucco is angry at everything, and you happen to be closest.

Wanderer customs that grew around it:

- You do not test whether a chicken is a Cucco. You assume, and you walk wide.
- Killing a Cucco is legal, survivable, and still considered tempting fate.
- Calling a person "a Cucco" means small, furious, and not worth the fight they will absolutely give you.

## Cucco Rage

Eating the raw meat of an aggressive chicken always causes **Cucco Rage**, the only disease in Corpus that is guaranteed on exposure. The canonical description:

> Raw meat from a bird that wanted you dead. You run hotter and hit harder, then the flock takes the wheel.

In fiction, the bird's fury outlives the bird. Whatever burns in a Cucco does not care that its body is gone; it rides the meat into whoever eats it. Early symptoms feel like a gift (speed, strength, aggression), which is exactly how it recruits. Then Flock Frenzy and Peck Madness set in and the eater stops steering.

Cooked properly, the same fury is domesticated into the Cucco Fury, Cucco Chase, and Cucco Vigor buffs. Wanderer cooks are quietly proud of this: the angriest animal in Corpus, reduced to a good dinner.

## Lore connections

The Cucco is a soft echo of the ladder (see [`world/the-ladder.md`](../world/the-ladder.md)): whatever burns in these birds has been through more lives than a chicken should get, and it survives death out of what looks like spite. Keep the connection implicit; no copy should call a Cucco an old climber outright. The Cucco works best as a joke that is also, if you look at it straight, slightly alarming.

## Writing rules

- Cucco content can be funny. It is the one place in the bible where comedy is the default register.
- Never make the Cucco safe. The joke depends on the threat being real.
- The word "Cucco" is wanderer slang for aggressive chickens specifically; ordinary chickens are just chickens.
